Nursery rhymes:
- 100-140 BPM
- rhythm:
	- 2-4 stressed syllables per line -> 2-4 beats per line
	- trochaic or iambic rhythm pattern
	- AABB or ABAB rhyme scheme

- melody:
	- within an octave
	- major keys
	- stepwise motion (one or two note disparity between beats)
	- finish with the tonic (home note)
	- Follow a rise than fall pattern.

- instruments: piano, acoustic guitar, ukulele, xylophone, tambourines, hand drums, recorder, fiddle


Hip-hop music:
**Hip-hop music:**
- 85-95 BPM
- rhythm:
	- 4/4 time signature
	- kick drum on beats 1 and 3
	- snare on beats 2 and 4
	- hi-hat sixteenth-note patterns with triplet fills
- melody/harmony:
	- minor keys
	- 2-4 chord loops (i-bVII-bVI-V or i-iv-bVII-bIII)
	- sampled melodic loops (4-8 bars)
- instruments/sounds:
	- 808 bass/sub-bass
	- drum machine (TR-808 or TR-909 sounds)
	- sampled piano, strings, or synth pads
	- vocal samples/chops
- structure:
	- intro (4-8 bars) - verse (16 bars) - hook (8 bars) - verse (16 bars) - hook (8 bars) - bridge (8 bars) - hook (8 bars) - outro (4-8 bars)

Rock music:

- 120-140 BPM
- Rhythm:
	- 4/4 time signature
	- Backbeat on beats 2 and 4 (snare)
	- Eighth-note hi-hat patterns
- Melody/Harmony:

	- Minor keys preferred
	- Power chords (root and fifth intervals)
	- Pentatonic scale for melodies
	- I-IV-V or I-bVII-IV chord progressions
- Instruments:
	- Electric guitar (distorted)
	- Bass guitar
	- Drum kit (kick, snare, hi-hat, crash cymbal)
	- Vocals
- Structure:
	- Verse (16 bars) - Chorus (8 bars) - Verse (16 bars) - Chorus (8 bars) - Bridge (8 bars) - Chorus (8 bars)
	- Guitar solo in bridge section
